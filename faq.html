Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your visit to Mauna Kea Wellness Spa.

What is lomi lomi massage and how is it different from Swedish massage?+
Lomi lomi is a traditional Hawaiian healing massage that uses long, flowing, continuous strokes — often using the forearms as well as hands — to work the entire body in rhythmic waves. Unlike Swedish massage, which tends to work one area at a time, lomi lomi moves continuously and holistically. It also incorporates an energetic, spiritual dimension through intention-setting and breathwork. The experience is deeply meditative and often profoundly emotional. Many guests describe it as unlike anything they have experienced before.
What should I expect during my first visit?+
When you arrive, you will be warmly welcomed and offered water or tea in our relaxation lounge. Your therapist will invite you for a brief consultation to discuss your health history, any areas of focus or concern, and your intentions for the session. For lomi lomi sessions, we begin with a brief pule (intention-setting ceremony). You will be professionally draped throughout the session and your comfort and privacy are our highest priority. Afterward, please allow 15–20 minutes in the lounge to allow the healing to settle before driving.
Do I need to undress for the massage?+
For optimal results with lomi lomi and most body massage services, we recommend undressing to your comfort level. You will always be professionally draped with sheets, and only the area being worked will be uncovered. Many guests prefer to keep underwear on — this is completely fine. For lomi lomi, some guests choose to wear minimal coverage to allow the full-body, continuous stroke work — your therapist will never pressure you in any direction. Your comfort determines your experience.
Are there health conditions that might prevent me from receiving a massage?+
Certain conditions require modification or avoidance of specific techniques. These include active infections or skin conditions in the treatment area, blood clots or deep vein thrombosis, recent surgery, cancer (depending on stage and treatment status), and severe osteoporosis. Pregnancy is not a contraindication — we offer specialized prenatal massage. Please disclose all relevant health information during your intake so we can customize your session for safety and maximum benefit. When in doubt, consult your physician before booking.
How far in advance should I book my session?+
We recommend booking at least one week in advance, especially if you have a specific therapist or time preference. Our most popular times — weekend mornings and weekday evenings — often fill two to three weeks ahead, particularly during holiday seasons. For couples packages and full-day retreats, two to four weeks advance booking is advisable. Same-day appointments are occasionally available — call us at (808) 555-0125 to check availability.
What is your cancellation policy?+
We request 24 hours notice for individual session cancellations and 48 hours for couples packages and full-day retreats. Cancellations within this window may be subject to a 50% service fee, as our therapists rely on scheduled appointments for their income. We understand that unexpected circumstances arise — please call us as early as possible and we will do our best to accommodate you. First-time no-shows will be required to provide a credit card to book future appointments.
Do you offer gift certificates?+
Yes! Gift certificates are one of our most popular offerings and are available for any dollar amount or specific service. They can be purchased in person or by calling our spa. Gift certificates are valid for one year from purchase date and can be used toward any service, upgrade, or product. We also offer beautifully packaged gift sets that include a certificate with our signature botanical products — perfect for birthdays, anniversaries, or any occasion that calls for the gift of healing.
Where are you located and how do I get there from Kailua-Kona?+
We are located at 65-1158 Mamalahoa Highway in Waimea, approximately 40–45 minutes from Kailua-Kona via Highway 190. From Hilo, we are about 55 minutes via Highway 19. From the Kohala Coast resorts, we are approximately 30 minutes south on Highway 19/270. Ample free parking is available. The drive itself — through the dramatic landscape transition from coastal lava fields to cool green highlands — is part of the arriving experience. We recommend leaving a little early to allow for a mindful transition.
What botanicals and oils do you use? Are they locally sourced?+
We are committed to using locally sourced, sustainable ingredients whenever possible. Our primary massage medium is organic cold-pressed coconut oil from a family farm on Hawaii Island. We also use kukui nut oil (the traditional Hawaiian healing oil), macadamia nut oil, and a rotating selection of locally wildcrafted botanical infusions including noni, ti leaf, and mamaki. Our facial treatments use Hawaiian botanical extracts alongside professional-grade European skincare lines. We never use synthetic fragrances — all aromatherapy is from pure essential oils or natural botanical sources.
Is tipping customary?+
Gratuities are warmly appreciated but never expected. If you feel moved to express appreciation for your therapist's work, the customary range is 15–20% of the service price. Cash gratuities go directly to your therapist. You are also welcome to express appreciation through a kind review on Google or by referring friends and family — these gestures mean a great deal to our small team and to the healing traditions we are working to sustain and share.
